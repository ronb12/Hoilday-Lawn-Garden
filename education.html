Lawn Care Education & Tips

Expert advice and practical tips to help you maintain a healthy, beautiful lawn year-round. Learn from the professionals at Holliday's Lawn & Garden.

🌱 Seasonal Lawn Care Guide

Your lawn has different needs throughout the year. Follow this seasonal guide to keep your grass healthy and vibrant.

Spring (March - May)

  • Rake up winter debris
  • Apply pre-emergent weed control
  • Start mowing when grass reaches 3 inches
  • Fertilize with slow-release formula
  • Check irrigation system

Summer (June - August)

  • Water deeply but infrequently
  • Mow at higher setting (3-4 inches)
  • Apply summer fertilizer
  • Watch for pests and diseases
  • Avoid mowing during drought

Fall (September - November)

  • Continue mowing until growth stops
  • Apply fall fertilizer
  • Rake leaves regularly
  • Overseed thin areas
  • Winterize irrigation system

Winter (December - February)

  • Keep off frozen grass
  • Remove heavy snow carefully
  • Plan spring maintenance
  • Service lawn equipment
  • Monitor for winter damage

✂️ Mowing Best Practices

Proper mowing is crucial for lawn health. Follow these guidelines for professional results.

Mowing Height

Keep grass at 3-4 inches tall. Taller grass develops deeper roots and shades out weeds.

One-Third Rule

Never remove more than one-third of the grass blade length in a single mowing.

Sharp Blades

Sharp mower blades create clean cuts that heal quickly and prevent disease.

Mowing Pattern

Change your mowing direction each time to prevent grass from leaning in one direction.

Dry Grass

Mow when grass is dry to prevent clumping and ensure even cutting.

Leave Clippings

Leave grass clippings on the lawn - they provide natural fertilizer and don't cause thatch.

⚠️ Common Mowing Mistakes

• Mowing too short (scalping)
• Mowing wet grass
• Using dull blades
• Mowing too infrequently

💧 Watering Guidelines

Proper watering is essential for a healthy lawn. Learn when and how much to water.

Watering Schedule

Water deeply 2-3 times per week rather than daily light watering. This encourages deep root growth.

Early Morning Watering

Water between 6-10 AM to minimize evaporation and prevent fungal diseases.

Water Amount

Apply 1-1.5 inches of water per week, including rainfall. Use a rain gauge to measure.

Signs of Under-Watering

Grass blades fold, footprints remain visible, and grass turns bluish-gray.

Signs of Over-Watering

Excessive thatch, fungal growth, and shallow root systems.

Drought Response

During drought, grass may go dormant. This is normal and it will green up when rain returns.

🌿 Fertilization Guide

Proper fertilization provides essential nutrients for healthy grass growth and development.

Soil Testing

Test your soil every 2-3 years to determine nutrient needs and pH levels.

NPK Ratio

Look for balanced fertilizers (e.g., 16-4-8) with nitrogen for growth, phosphorus for roots, and potassium for health.

Slow-Release Fertilizer

Use slow-release formulas that feed grass gradually over 6-8 weeks.

Application Timing

Fertilize when grass is actively growing, typically spring and fall for cool-season grasses.

Even Application

Use a spreader and apply fertilizer evenly to prevent burning and uneven growth.

Water After Application

Water lightly after fertilizing to activate the nutrients and prevent burning.

✅ Fertilization Schedule

Spring: Apply when grass starts growing (March-April)
Summer: Light application if needed (June)
Fall: Most important application (September-October)
Winter: No fertilization needed

🌱 Weed Control Strategies

Prevent and control weeds with these effective strategies for a weed-free lawn.

Pre-Emergent Herbicides

Apply in early spring before weed seeds germinate to prevent annual weeds.

Post-Emergent Herbicides

Use selective herbicides to kill existing weeds without harming grass.

Manual Removal

Pull weeds by hand when soil is moist, ensuring you remove the entire root system.

Healthy Lawn Prevention

A thick, healthy lawn naturally prevents weeds by crowding them out.

Proper Mowing

Mowing at the correct height prevents weed seeds from getting sunlight to germinate.

Regular Maintenance

Address weed problems early before they spread and become difficult to control.

⚠️ Weed Control Safety

• Always read and follow herbicide labels
• Apply on calm days to prevent drift
• Keep children and pets off treated areas
• Store herbicides safely out of reach

🔍 Common Lawn Problems & Solutions

Identify and solve common lawn problems before they become serious issues.

Brown Patches

Usually caused by drought, disease, or pests. Check soil moisture and look for signs of disease or insect damage.

Thin Grass

Overseed in fall, improve soil quality, and ensure proper watering and fertilization.

Mushrooms

Indicate excess moisture. Improve drainage and reduce watering frequency.

Thatch Buildup

Remove excess thatch with a dethatcher in early spring or fall.

Compacted Soil

Aerate in spring or fall to improve root growth and water penetration.

Grub Damage

Apply grub control in late spring or early summer to prevent damage.
